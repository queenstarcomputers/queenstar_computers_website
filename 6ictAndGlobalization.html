How ICT Has Impacted Globalization

Queenstar Computers: 15 Dec, 2023 | Category>> Tech Trends

Globalization is a term used to refer to the growing interaction and integration of the world’s economies, cultures, governments, and populations, due to technology, cross-border trade, and flows of investment, people, and information. From the term globalization, we can get technological globalization which refers to the increasing speed of technological diffusion across the global economy, especially from developed to developing nations. The ever-increasing impact of Information and Communications Technologies (ICT) on globalization can be evidenced by the high rate of innovation, rapid application of recent scientific advances in new products and processes, rising skill requirements, and a shift to industries and services that are more knowledge-intensive.

Impact on Education

Within a very short period, ICT has become, one of the primary building blocks of modern education in the global society. ICTs have enabled interconnection and interdependency between education and the globalized world. For instance, students can enroll in degree programs in foreign universities and learn online through videoconferencing using applications such as Zoom and Teams.

Impact on Mobile Banking

With ICT people can access money through mobile banking both locally and around the world at a faster pace than ever. For example, people working in foreign nations such as America can send money to their kin back at home using mobile money transfer technologies such as PayPal, Remitly, WorldRemit, and WesternUnion, among others. With digital technologies such as TransferWise, we can speed up international currency conversion rates. Also, smartphone stock trading apps facilitate the ownership of capital to be shuffled around the world at a faster rate than ever, thus helping to speed up globalization and lowering the start-up capital cost to facilitate the development of new businesses.

Impact on the Media

The most visible effect of globalization is widespread communication. With the introduction of the internet, media houses are now able to convey information in a cheaper, faster, and more efficient manner to their intended audience all over the world. Also, they can transfer information in real-time globally. The media have a crucial impact on culture through the provision of an extensive transnational transmission of cultural products and the formation of social structures and communication networks.

Impact on Online Communities

A lot of people nowadays have access to the internet which makes it possible for them to develop contacts with others with whom they share values, interests, and hobbies around the world. This has in turn enabled people to ‘find their tribe’ and helped them to develop their identities uniquely. For instance, social media platforms such as Facebook, WhatsApp, Instagram, and Twitter, among others.

There is no doubt that ICT has been, and will forever remain a significant driver of globalization in the future.
