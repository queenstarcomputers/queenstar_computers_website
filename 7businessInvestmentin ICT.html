Why are businesses investing so much in information systems and technology?

Queenstar Computers: 19 Dec, 2023 | Category>> BizTech

Information systems are essential for conducting day to day business in our organizations. Today’s service industries – finance, insurance, and real estate as well as personal services like travel, medicines and education- could not operate without information systems. Just like offices, telephones, filing cabinets and efficient tall buildings with the elevators, were once the foundations of business in the 20th century. Information technology is a foundation for business in the 21st century. Increasing market share, becoming the high quality or low-cost producer, developing new products and increasing employee’s productivity depend more and more on the kinds and quality of information systems in the organization. Some of the major reasons why businesses are investing a lot in information systems and technology include:

Operational excellence

Businesses improve efficiency of their operation in order to achieve higher profitability. Information systems and technologies are some of important tools available to managers for achieving high levels of efficiency and productivity in business operations, especially when coupled with changes in business practices and management behaviour. For example, in a retail shop, as soon as a customer purchases an item, the supplier monitoring the item knows hence ship a replacement to the shelf.

New products, services and business models

Information systems and technology are major enabling tools for firms to create new products or services as well as entirely business models. A business model describes how a company produces, delivers and sells a product or services to create wealth. In music industry for instance, Apple Inc, transformed an old business model of music distribution based on vinyl records, tapes and CDs into online, legal distribution model based on its own iPod technology platform. Apple has prospered from a continuing stream of iPod innovations, including the iPod, the iTunes music services and the iPhones.

Customer and supplier intimacy

When a business serves its customers well, the customers will respond by returning and purchasing more. This raises revenues and profits likewise with suppliers. The more the business engages it suppliers the better the suppliers can provide vital inputs and this lowers cost. For example, in the hotel industry, computers are used to keep track of guests’ preferences, such as their preferred room temperature, check in time, frequently dialled telephone numbers and TV programmes and store these data in a giant data repository. With the suppliers, an information system updates as follows; every time an item is bought from a given store, the record of the sales appears immediately on supplier’s computer. Through the computer model, it decides how many replacements items to make and in what colour, models, sizes and styles. It then supplies to its stores.

Improved decision making

Information systems enable managers to use real time data from the market place when making decision instead of relying on guesses and luck. For example, managers use of web based digital dashboard to provide them with precise real time information on customers’ complaints, network performance for each locality served and line outages or storm damaged lines. Managers use this information to immediately allocate repair resources to affected areas, inform customers of repair efforts and restore services fast.

Competitive advantage

It is providing a product or service in a way that customers value more than what the competition is able to do. When organizations achieve one or more of these business objectives- operational excellence, new products or services and business models; customers/suppliers’ intimacy; and improved decision making- chances are they have already achieved a competitive advantage. For example, charging less for superior products and responding to customers and suppliers in real time all add up to higher sales and profits that your competitors cannot match.

Survival

Information systems and technology are necessity of doing business which at times is driven by industry-level changes. For example, providing ATM services to retail banking customers is simply a requirement of being in and surviving in the retail banking business.
